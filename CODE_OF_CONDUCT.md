# Code of conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in Game Design Orchestrator respectful, safe, and productive for people of every background, identity, experience level, discipline, and perspective.

Game design discussions regularly involve taste, accessibility, culture, player behavior, failure, and criticism. Challenge ideas and evidence rigorously while treating contributors and test participants with care.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and constructive.
- Critique a claim, artifact, test, or consequence rather than a person.
- State assumptions and uncertainty instead of presenting preference as fact.
- Preserve material disagreement without misrepresenting another contributor's position.
- Respect privacy, confidentiality, attribution, and intellectual property.
- Use inclusive language and consider accessibility in both the project and its community.
- Accept responsibility, apologize when appropriate, and repair harm where possible.
- Follow maintainer requests intended to protect participants or keep a discussion on topic.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Discriminatory, sexualized, demeaning, or personally insulting language or imagery.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Pressuring anyone to disclose identity, employment, health, demographic, or playtest information.
- Fabricating player evidence, quotations, authorship, test results, or reviewer independence.
- Deliberately misrepresenting a contributor's work or using review as retaliation.
- Sharing confidential game material, security reports, credentials, or player data publicly.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional community.

## Scope

This code applies in repository discussions, issues, pull requests, reviews, project-managed communication spaces, and public situations where someone represents the project or its community.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to repository maintainers through an available private GitHub channel. For a security issue, use [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). Do not post sensitive evidence in a public issue.

Maintainers will review reports as confidentially as practical, consider the safety and preferences of affected people, and avoid conflicts of interest. Anyone directly involved in a report should recuse themselves from its resolution where possible.

Proportionate actions may include:

1. A private clarification or correction request.
2. A formal warning and conditions for continued participation.
3. Temporary restriction from project spaces.
4. Permanent removal from project spaces.

Immediate restriction may be used when necessary to protect participants. Retaliation against a reporter or participant in an investigation is itself a violation.
